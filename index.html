  • Darce Choke
    ★ ★ ★ ☆ ☆

    The Darce choke is, in essence, an anaconda choke done on the other side. Both of these chokes originate from the arm triangle (kata-gatame) and share its mechanics. The principles behind the Darce are that you’re using your arms to create a triangle around the opponent’s neck, cutting out the blood flow to the brain. One arm is placed in a V-shape at the front of the neck while trapping the opponent’s shoulder. The palm of this arm is then placed in the crease of the opposite arm’s elbow, grabbing the biceps. this creates the triangle shape that is essential to controlling the position.
